    Was Yung Joc Arrested? What Happened?

    Yung Joc, also known as Jasiel Amon Robinson, was arrested and booked into Gwinnett County Jail on a child abandonment charge.

    Initially, details surrounding the arrest were scarce, and the rapper remained silent about the circulating story. However, he eventually broke his silence during his radio segment on Streetz Morning Takeover for Streetz 94.5 FM.

    According to Yung Joc’s explanation, he was pulled over by officers from the Atlanta Police Department while driving home to prepare for an event. A female officer identified him, leading to another officer asking him to leave the car. 

    Subsequently, he was handcuffed and informed that there might be a warrant for his arrest. He was then transported to the station for booking.

    Yung Joc paid a $1,300 bond and was released approximately two hours after the arrest.

    Yung Joc confirmed that the abandonment charge was related to child support payments. However, he vehemently denied any abandonment, stating that he had consistently paid his child’s mother, whose name he did not disclose. 

    He said he was paying her between four to five thousand dollars a month, going above and beyond to ensure financial support for his child. 

    Joc explained that he had decided to take a step back to handle other financial obligations, including upcoming wedding expenses, which upset the child’s mother.

    As tensions escalated, the radio host suggested involving lawyers to create an official child support contract and establish a monthly payment amount. 

    Although negotiations were ongoing at the end of the year, Yung Joc offered his child’s mother $1,500 for Christmas shopping.

    However, she and her lawyer were reportedly dissatisfied with the amount, resulting in the abandonment charge against Yung Joc.

    The rapper was surprised at the warrant, as he had been unaware of its existence. He emphasized that he had never abandoned his child or neglected child support obligations.

    Intending to reverse the charge and clear his name, Yung Joc anticipated resolving the matter in court. He has eight children, two sets of twins, and four different women.
