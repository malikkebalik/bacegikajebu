    Early Life and Education- What Nationality Is Post Malone?

    Post Malone, AKA Austin Richard Post, was born on July 4, 1995, in Syracuse, New York. Post Malone grew up with his father, Rich Post, and his step-mother, Jodie. Since his father was a Disc jockey, Post got into hip-hop, country, and rock music at an early age.

    Talking about his ethnicity, Post belongs to white ethnicity and holds an American nationality. Likewise, when he was just nine years old, the Post family moved to Grapevine, Texas. There he completed his high school from Grapevine High School and pursued higher studies from Tarrant County College. However, he dropped out.

    Career: Beginnings and ‘Rockstar’

    From an early age, Post Malone loved emo music, which still influences his music now, though they are more on softer and hip-hop styles. The young rapper started his music journey at 16 when he created his first mixtape Young After Them, Riches, with Audacity. 

    Following his passion for music, Post moved to Los Angeles, California, with his friend Jason Probst. There he met 1st and Rich from FKi and Rex Kudo, who helped produce many of Post’s tracks, including ‘White Iverson.’ Post released the music video on July 19, 2015, for which he received many acclaims including one from Mac Miller and Wiz Khalifa.

    The song helped Post establish himself in the music industry, and as expected, the young, emerging artist signed a recording contract with Republic Records. With just that, Post’s music reach became wider, and he got to work with many prominent rappers such as 50 Cent, Kanye West, and Young Thug.

    Eventually, Post became the leading act for Justin Bieber‘s Purpose World Tour in 2017, exposing him to an even bigger audience. Following the event and his on-going success, Post Malone released the first full-length mixtape, titled August 26, followed by his other albums Stoney(2016) and Beerbongs & Bentleys(2018).

    ‘Rockstar,’ one of his first single from the album Beerbongs & Bentleys, became one of his critically acclaimed and successful single to date.

    The song peaked at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 for eight consecutive weeks. Soon after, his other songs, ‘Psycho‘ and ‘Sunflower,’ followed the same success.

    Moreover, in July 2019, he released another track called Goodbyes with Young Thug, followed by another single Circle. On September 6, 2019, Post released his third studio album titled ‘Hollywood’s Bleeding,‘ which debuted at number one on the Billboard 2000, selling more than 480,000 in its first week.

    Awards and Achievements

    The young rapper and songwriter has swayed many fans and audiences with his songs. Apart from that, the lyrical genius has bagged many records and achievements.

    In 2017, Post Malone was tagged as one of the most famous musicians in the country by Rolling Stone. His album Beerbongs & Bentley broke the first-day streaming records on Spotify with over 78.7 million streams worldwide. So, the album was so much played and successful that it was certified by the RIAA after just four days.

    But it was just the beginning for the young genius as he continued breaking more records. In August, Post broke Michael Jackson‘s 34-year-old record for most weeks on Billboard’s top R&B and Hip-hop Albums Chart. As a matter of fact, Stoney spent 77 weeks on the chart compared to the 76 weeks by Thriller.

    Though Post has been described as ‘melting pot of the country, grunge, hip-hop, and R&B,’ Malone describes his music as genre-less.

    So far, Post Malone has been nominated for four awards at the 61st Grammy Awards for his album Beerbongs & Bentleys, including Album of the Year and Record of the Year. Also, he even performed with Red Hot Chilli Peppers on February 10, 2019.
