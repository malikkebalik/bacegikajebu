    WWE is the world’s most beloved wrestling show ever in entertainment history.

    Besides, huge universal fans still treasure the show till now, and the craze for the delightful production is forever to exist for over millennia.

    Throughout the hilarious journey of epic production, innumerable fighting personas won global hearts through their splendid performances in the show.

    So, through this article, I present you with the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time who prospered along the spectacular trip.

    You may already know who is going to pop out on the list. Some of them may be your all-time favorite wrestling stars.

    So, without further ado, let’s jump right into the article and check them out!

    Top 10 Greatest WWE Wrestlers of All Time

    The top ten greatest WWE wrestlers of all time are enlisted below.

    The list is compiled through various reliable sites such as GiveMeSport.

    So, let’s dig in!

    10. Randy Orton

    Among the ten greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, Randy Orton managed to add up his name.

    Randal Keith Orton, professionally known as Randy Orton, is a renowned wrestler and actor from Missouri, U.S.

    Randy Orton was born on April 1, 1980, in Tennessee, USA.

    Orton debuted at WWF (World Wrestling Federation), now WWE, on April 25, 2002.

    Likewise, he is a former member of the professional wrestling stable Evolution along with Batista, Triple H, and Ric Flair.

    During a plotline in the WWE, he savagely disrespected and brutally attacked the WWE Hall of Famers.

    From then, Randy was labeled as The Legend Killer.

    At first, Orton won World Heavyweight Champion just at the age of 24.

    Orton won several titles throughout his fighting career, such as the 18th overall Grand Slam Champion, 17th Triple Crown Champion, and two-times Money in the Bank Ladder Match winner.

    Also, he is a ten times WWE Champion and four times World Heavy Weight Champion.

    Moreover, Randy is acknowledged as the third most world championship winner(14 wins) after John Cena and Ric Flair (both 16 wins).

    Interesting Facts: Randy Orton’s most jaw-dropping move is RKO. Furthermore, his rivalry with John Cena is one of the greatest and longest rivalries ever in WWE history.

    9. Kane

    Among the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, Kane managed to add his name in the ninth place.

    Glenn Thomas Jacobs, professionally known as Kane, is a wrestler, actor, and politician.

    Besides, he was born on April 26, 1967, in Torrejon de Adoz, Spain.

    Initially, Kane made his first debut on WWE Raw on June 25, 1995.

    Similarly, Jacobs played numerous characters till he was victoriously entitled as Kane.

    Likewise, he aided Undertaker to succeed in the first-ever Hell in a Cell match with Shawn Michaels.

    During that time, Jacobs utilized Undertaker’s Tombstone Piledriver.

    Moreover, the episode was entitled  Badd Blood: In Your House. Also, it aired on October 5, 1997.

    Since then, he is noted as Kane in the WWE.

    Furthermore, Kane is a three-time World Champion, two-time Intercontinental Champion, and 12- time World Tag Team Champion.

    Interesting Facts: Kane headlined the WWE Hall of Fame of Class 2021.

    8. Triple H

    Among the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, Triple H managed to add his name at eighth place.

    Paul Michael Levesque, celebrated by Triple H’s ring name, is an American professional wrestler, actor, and entrepreneur.

    Triple H was born on July 27, 1969, in Nashua, New Hampshire.

    At first, Triple H debuted on WWF(now WWE) on February 1, 1994.

    Likewise, in 1999, he won his first WWF Championship.

    Since then, Triple H is renowned as the best wrestler in North America.

    Similarly, he headlined numerous WWE prime pay-per-view events.

    Moreover, Triple H won several titles such as 14 times World Champion, 5-time Intercontinental Champion, three-time World Tagteam Champion, two times Royal Rumble match winner, and two-time European Champion.

    Interesting Facts: Triple H is noted for producing the association’s promotional branch NXT.

    7. Hulk Hogan

    Among the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, Hulk Hogan managed to add his name at seventh place.

    Popular by his ring name Hulk Hogan, Terry Eugene Bollea is a former American pro wrestler and television personality.

    Hulk Hogan was born on August 11, 1953, in Augusta, US.

    At first, Hogan debuted on WWF in 1979.

    During his initial period, Hogan won the WWF Championship five times.

    Similarly, he was the first wrestler to win the Royal Rumble match consecutively held in 1990 and 1991.

    Likewise, Hogan won numerous titles throughout his fighting career, including six times WCW World Heavyweight Champion and three times valuable WCW pay-per-view star at Starrcade 97′.

    Moreover, the WWE Hall of Fame inducted Hogan as a member in 2005.

    Sadly, Hogan retired from his fighting career in 2012.

    6. Ric Flair

    Among the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, Ric Flair managed to add his name at sixth place.

    Richard Morgan Fliehr, renowned by his ring name Ric Flair is a former American professional wrestler and wrestling manager.

    Ric Flair was born on February 25, 1949, in Tennessee, US.

    At first, Flair debuted on WWE on December 10, 1972.

    Besides, he is celebrated for his tenures with WWF (now WWE), Jim Crockett Promotions (JCP), World Championship Wrestling (WCW), and Total Nonstop Action Wrestling.

    Similarly, Flair is labeled as The Nature Boy since the mid-70s.

    In addition, Flair won several titles throughout his fighting career, including 16 times World Champion (two times WWF Champion, six times WCW World Heavyweight Champion, and eight times NWA World Heavyweight Champion).

    Moreover, he starred in the prime annual NWA/ WCW event Starrcade for ten episodes.

    Furthermore, PMW awarded Flair as six times Wrestler of the Year.

    The iconic wrestler of all-time retired from his career on December 3, 2012.

    Interesting Facts: Ric Flair is also a member of the Professional Wrestling Hall of Fame, Wrestling Hall of Fame, NWA Hall of Fame, and The Four Horsemen.

    5. John Cena

    Among the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, John Cena managed to add his name at fifth place.

    John Cena is a renowned wrestler, actor, and former rapper.

    John Cena was born on April 23, 1997, in West Newbury, US.

    At first, he debuted on WWF (later WWE) in June 2002.

    Similarly, Cena was notable after he played the persona of the trash-talking rapper.

    Likewise, his breakthrough started when he won WWE Championship for the first time in 2005.

    After that, Cena was celebrated as the franchise player and the public’s beloved wrestling star.

    Throughout his wrestling journey, Cena won numerous titles such as 16 times World Champion, 2 times Royal Rumble match winner, 13 times WWE Champion, 4 times World Tagteam Champion, 5 times Wrestlemania headliner, and 5 times United States Champion.

    4. Shawn Michaels

    Among the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, Shawn Michaels managed to add his name at fourth place.

    Shawn Michaels, renowned by his ring name Mr. Wrestlemania is a former American professional wrestler, television host, and actor.

    Shawn Michaels was born on July 22, 1965, in Chandler, US.

    At first, he debuted on WWE on October 8, 1984.

    Similarly, Shawn was the co-founder and leader of the Attitude Era’s most victorious stable D-Generation X.

    Likewise, he was two times inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame. Firstly, in 2011 as an independent wrestler. Later, as D-Generation X members along with Triple H, Chyna in 2019.

    From 1989 to 2018, Michaels also headlined pay-per-view events and WrestleMania five times.

    Moreover, Michaels is a four-time world champion, eleven times Pro Wrestling Illustrated award winner, two times Royal Rumble winner, first WWF Grand Slam Champion, and fourth WWF Triple Crown Champion.

    Furthermore, he is also two times American Wrestling Association World Tagteam Champion and a one-time Intercontinental Champion.

    Sadly, the iconic fighting star retired from his wrestling career on March 28, 2010, due to a back injury.

    Interesting Facts: Michaels’s match against John Cena was noted as WWE’s finest match. Besides, it was debuted on the association’s flagship Raw on April 23, 2007.

    3. Steve Austin, aka Stone Cold

    Among the top ten greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, Steve Austin managed to add his name at third place.

    Steve Austin, celebrated by his ring name, Stone, is a retired American pro wrestler, actor, and television presenter.

    Steve Austin was born on December 18, 1964, in Austin, Texas.

    At first, Steve debuted on WWF on September 30, 1989.

    Similarly, Austin was noted as the Poster Boy of the Attitude era.

    Throughout his wrestling career, Stone Cold won several titles such as two times WWF Intercontinental Champion, four-time WWF Tagteam Champion, six times WWF Champion, one time Million Dollar Champion, three times Royal Rumble winner, and fifth triple crown champion.

    Likewise, he also won the titles of two-time WCW World Television Champion, two times World Heavyweight Champion, and one time NWA World Tagteam Champion.

    Also, Steve won the title of WWF King of Ring tournament in 1996. Since then, he is prominent as a Ringmaster of WWE.

    Later, Stone Cold retired from his wrestling career on March 30, 2003.

    Interesting Facts: Steve Austin was an inductee of WWE Hall of Fame in 2009. Furthermore, he headlined numerous WWE pay-per-view events.

    2. Dwayne Johnson

    Among the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time, Dwayne Johnson managed to add his name at second place.

    Dwayne Johnson, renowned by his ring name The Rock, is an American actor, retired pro wrestler, and entrepreneur.

    Dwayne Johnson was born on May 2, 1972, in Hayward, California.

    At first, Johnson debuted for WWF (now WWE) in 1996.

    Similarly, he was noted for his gimmick as a charming, boastful trash-talker.

    In 1998, Rock won his first WWF Championship, becoming the prime world champion of African-American descent.

    In addition, Dwayne won numerous titles during his wrestling journeys, such as ten times world champion, five times Tag Team Champion, 2000 Royal Rumble winner, two times Intercontinental Champion, and WWE’s sixth Triple Crown champion.

    Moreover, Rock was the spotlight of WWE’s two flagship programs, Raw and Smackdown.

    Furthermore, WWE headlined Dwayne’s Wrestlemania XXVIII as the best-selling pay-per-view of all time.

    Sadly, he retired from his wrestling career on August 3, 2019.

    1. The Undertaker

    As the leading wrestler among the top 10 greatest WWE wrestlers of all time comes none other than the legendary Undertaker.

    Mark William Calaway, celebrated by the ring name The Undertaker, is a retired spectacular wrestler of WWE.

    Mark William Calaway was born on March 24, 1965, in Houston, US.

    At first, Calaway debuted on WWF on November 19, 1990.

    Similarly, Undertaker was one of the dominant wrestlers for promoting the Attitude Era in the late 90s.

    Likewise, he is noted for The Streak, a series of 21 straight victories, his finest performance ever at WWE’s major annual event, Wrestlemania.

    In addition, Undertaker won several titles during his wrestling career, such as 6 times World Tag team champion, 3 times World Heavyweight champion, 4 times WWF champion, and one time WWE Hardcore champion.

    Moreover, he headlined as one of the splendid pay-per-view performers in WWE, including premieres on Wrestlemania five times.

    The GOAT in WWE’s history retired from his fighting career on November 22, 2020. During that occasion, he waved farewell to the entire WWE family in the Survivor Series event.
