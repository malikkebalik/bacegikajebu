    Hulu Wedding Season Actor Gavin Drea Wiki | Bio

    Gavin Drea (born in 1993) is a London-based 29 years old Irish actor who is known for his performances in productions like “Resistance” and “Rebellion.” He also played a prominent role in the 2016 series “Barbarians Rising,” where his role was of Alaric.

    Drea is the one who gave his voice to the lead character of the famous video game Cyber Punk. Yes, you read right, Gavin worked as the voice artist in one of the most popular games of the 20th century.

    In 2022, this Irish artist gained popularity after landing the lead role in Hulu’s series “Wedding Season.” Gavin played Stefan alongside Rosa Salazar, who played Katie, the lead female character. “Wedding Season” is an action comedy where director George Kane filmed Gav and Rosa alongside artists like George Webster, Jade Harrison, Bhav Joshi, and Callie Cooke.


    How old is Gavin Drea?

    In 1993 Gavin Drea (age 29) took birth at his parents’ home in Dublin, Ireland. The father and mother of Gavin avoid being in the limelight, which is why there is no information present about them, although we found that the actor has two beautiful sisters, namely Lyndsey and Jenny. Lyndsey recently married her longtime lover, and Jenny is a professional model and actor, just like her elder brother.

    According to sources, the “Wedding Season” actor always wanted to be a theatre artist; infact, his journey in the entertainment industry started as a stage artist. He was a teenager when he joined a local theatre in Dublin.

    Gradually, he developed his drama skills and polished his vocal skills. Sources say that during his theatre days, he received a nomination for the Irish Times Theatre Award for Best Supporting Actor.


    Career Timeline of Gavin Drea

    The earliest on-screen performances of Drea involve short movies such as “Cruelty,” “Beirt Le Cheile,” and “The Spooky Pumpkin,” but only a few know that in 2012 he was in the Lenny Abrahamson-directed movie “What Richard Did” as Stephen.

    His first TV appearance was in 2010 when he appeared in a single episode of “Jack Taylor” as Christian Tracey. Although Gavin’s role in Stuart Orme’s show was not significant, it was good enough to bring him more opportunities. Following year Irish artist worked in Stuart Carolan’s “Love/Hate,” a Charlie Murphy starer show based on organized crime.

    With time Drea received more chances like “Éirí Amach Amú,” “Barbarians Rising,” “My Mother and Other Strangers,” and “Resistance.” On the one hand, where Gav was receiving fame for his work in the television industry, on the other hand, significant big-screen projects like “Valerian and the City of a Thousand Planets” got offered to him.

    He currently maintains a respectable place in the British and Irish acting industry. He experienced a significant boost in his popularity after he played the lead part in George Kane’s “Wedding Season.”
