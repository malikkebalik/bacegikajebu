    Before Blue Bloods spouses Jamie and Eddie were engaged and then married, there was another woman in Jamie’s life. The police sergeant almost married a woman named Sydney Davenport. Here’s what Showbiz Cheat Sheet knows about the actress who played Eddie’s fiancée and why the TV couple didn’t last.

    What happened between Jamie and his first fiancée, Sydney?

    Blue Bloods Season 1, Episode 1, opens with Jamie Reagan’s graduation from the police academy. A few moments after the graduation, we meet Sydney. It’s clear almost right away something isn’t right between Jamie and Sydney. They’re engaged, yet they haven’t set a date for their wedding. “We’d have another lawyer in the family if these two would set a date,” says Danny’s wife, Linda. “What? And spoil all the fun?” responds Sydney. We find out later a wedding date was never set because Sydney was having doubts about the relationship. We can also see how jealous Sydney gets when one of Jamie’s colleagues, officer Romano, stops by to congratulate him. Sydney doesn’t seem to trust Jamie.

    A big stressor for Sydney is the fear Jamie could be hurt or killed in the line of duty. She also wants to focus more on her law career, so she begins to drift away from Jamie. Unfortunately (well, fortunately for Eddie), things don’t work out and Sydney breaks up with Jamie.

    Who is the actress who plays Jamie’s first fiancée, Sydney?

    The actress who played Jamie’s fiancée is Dylan Moore. She made her film acting debut in the 2000 movie Tripfall, in which she played the role of Angela Williams. She made her television acting debut when she began playing Sydney Davenport in 2010. Moore remained on the show for five episodes. Her character was written out of the series in Blue Bloods Season 1, Episode 8, titled “China Town.”

    After Blue Bloods, Moore played Kelly in the 2011 film Opus. Her other acting roles include appearances in the movies The Submarine Kid, The Decades of Mason Carroll, and It’s Freezing Out There. As of this writing, Moore’s entertainment career also includes four producer credits. Her first production project was The Decades of Mason Carroll. Her other credits include the documentary I am a Seagull, the web series It’s Freezing Out Here, and the video short Half of It.

    What is Dylan Moore up to now?

    Moore, a 2009 Julliard graduate, is currently an executive producer, program director, and teacher at Artists Striving to End Poverty. She is also an associate producer and company manager at The Chekhov Project. In May 2013, Moore founded the production company Our Exquisite Corpse.
